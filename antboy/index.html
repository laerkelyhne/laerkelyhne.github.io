story

Pelle, a shy, awkward and chubby 12-year old boy develops superhuman powers, after he is infected with Hercules-toxin from a DNA modified ant. Pelle is suddenly gifted with the physical abilities of the tiny insect and is able to lift cars, climb walls and bite through solid steel. And oh yeah...he has also got acid for pee.

Aided by Wilhelm, the biggest comic book geek in Middletown, Pelle transforms into Antboy - a superhero, who fights crime and protects the innocent in the sleepy suburb.

But supervillains are not the only things that the acid peeing crime buster has to worry about. Pelle also has to tackle the every day challenges that face any middle school student; homework, chores, love, friendship and puberty.

Producer's note

Antboy originates from a bestselling Danish children’s book series by renowned author Kenneth Bøgh Andersen. Nimbus Film optioned the rights in 2008 with the ambition of creating the first ever Danish live action superhero in the shape of a young adolescent, the 12 year-old Pelle Nørhman. And hereby combining an international brand, all american genre and larger than life universe with strong, Danish storytelling tradition, humour and a hint of social realism. Antboy (2014) and Antboy: Revenge of the Red Fury (2015) achieved critical acclaim, box office success, are circulated on major film festivals and distributed internationally to over 80 countries. The third, and final film, to complete the trilogy, is in production and will be released in 2017.

Breaking new ground by venturing on to new platforms seems like the natural next step in developing the franchise and taking the Antboy universe to the next level. The series format allows us to unfold the characters, build on their backstories and extend their emotional arcs whilst adding new friends and foes to the arena for more fun and exciting adventures. Animation will allow us to generate more visual ideas and take the action sequences and physicality to another level, thereby exploring the special world and powers of our superhero universe even further.

As the Superhero genre itself originates from cartoons and graphic novels, there is a strong creative motivation to take on animation. Our head write Anders Ølholm and production designer Flemming Schmidt are both fanboys passionately dedicated to the superhero genre. As Anders is the principal writer on the Antboy live action trilogy as well, we are able to establish a strong narrative signature and coherent tone throughout the franchise.

About the show

“Antboy: Animated” is a superhero action comedy series. The show will, in part, be based on the three live-action feature films produced by Nimbus Film and will also expand upon the cinematic universe created in the trilogy. It won’t however be necessary for the audience to have seen any of the theatrical films in order to understand the characters or the story. To quote legendary comic book editor Stan Lee “every comic book is someone’s first”, a sentiment that the animated series will firmly integrate into the show. For example Antboy’s origin story will be explained in the opening title sequence of each episode.

The show will also be more episodic than serialized. Each episode is largely self-contained, but still moves the overall story forward. There will be some callbacks to earlier episodes, but the viewer can to tune in to an episode in the middle of the season and still be able to follow the plot.

Humor is a crucial part of the Antboy universe, that the animated series will take to a whole new level, injecting wacky comedy, self-referential jokes and breaking of the fourth wall into each episode – much like the groundbreaking animated series “Freakazoid!” by Paul Dini and Bruce Timm. The show will also reference other superhero movies, comic book mythology and popcultur in general. Even though the target demographic is the six to ten year age group, a lot of the humor will be enjoyable for adults as well.

Some episodes will tackle stories with a more serious thematic complexity and emotional depth, while other episodes will be more lighthearted and silly. “Antboy: Animated” will have simple, yet cool and stylized animation influenced by Japanese anime, much like “Teen Titans”.

ANTBOY

About

Being Ant Boy changed all that… kind of. He loves saving people and tussling with the forces of evil. But when his flashy costume comes off, he’s just plain old Weaver again.

The other kids have no idea that the famous super-hero Ant Boy is their classmate. Once in a while someone might point out a vague resemblance between the two, then shrug and change the subject. It can be hard for him to take part in regular teenager stuff. (You’re at a party, about to get up the nerve to ask a girl to dance, and suddenly you’re summoned to battle Cloudman on the top of the city’s tallest building.) Some kids think he’s just a bit flakey.

His parents haven’t clued in yet, either. They both work and they’re extremely busy – he’s pretty much a latchkey kid – and, while they love him, they’ve never found him to be all that interesting. Frankly, they’re pretty self-absorbed.

He’s still adjusting to his new abilities – especially when it comes to his regular life. Occasionally he wishes his powers included something like X-ray vision that he could use to help him with tests, or the ability to reverse time when he’s late for school. Mostly his powers come into play in gym class (he’s really good at chin-ups) and lunch (since his mom tends to overwrap his sandwiches.)

He’s still working on his super-hero quippery. It’s tricky enough hanging upside down from a bridge while wrestling with a crazed scientist who’s turned himself into a super-flea (all the while worrying about tomorrow’s math test) without having to come up with clever puns and put-downs. And – unfortunately, his ant powers come with one pesky drawback: he needs sugar. LOTS OF SUGAR. Which means in the middle of a battle with the bad guy he might need a time-out to stop and wolf down a couple of chocolate bars and wash it down with a Pepsi.

ALICIA DUFORT

About

When French business magnet Charles Richard Dufort acquired ExoFarm Industries, it was merely a way to eliminate the competition. The company was going to be shut down, when Alicia Dufort, the daughter of the wealthy businessman, deduced that ExoFarm was the source of Antboy’s powers.

Alicia took over the reigns of the corporation and immediately tried to lure Doctor Albert Gæmelkrå back into the fold and recreate his Hercules-serum, so that it could be used to cure terminal illnesses around the world. The doctor agreed to return, but when he discovered, that the C.E.O. only wanted to use Hercules for her own evil ends, he forged an uneasy (and short lived) alliance with Antboy to stop her and retrieve the serum.

Alicia Dufort is the mega villain of the Antboy universe. Unlike The Flea, who is inherently good, Alicia is pure evil. A spoiled rich girl, who tries to pass herself off as a philanthropist, but is actually a power hungry megalomaniac. She constantly attempts to use her extreme wealth to rid the world of Antboy – the only obstacle between her and world domination.

THE RED FURY

About

Maria Musajev lived in a public housing complex with her father, who fled their war-torn homeland, Kerznovia, when she was just a baby. Maria was a nerdy girl, who liked to read, who made her own clothes and had a special fondness for the color red. School, on the other hand, she wasn’t too crazy about. The other kids either saw right through her or worse…teased her incessantly. Maria didn’t really have anyone to talk to. Her mother died in the war, and her absentminded father, an unemployed science professor, was busy working on his invention, the lux-reflector; a technological device that could make certain materials bend light and render them seemingly invisible. If the darn thing would ever work!

One day the constant bullying became too much for Maria, and when she accidently got the lux-reflector to work, she stole the device and used it to get payback on all the kids at school, who had made her life miserable.

Antboy eventual stopped the invisible girl and made her see the error of her ways. The lux-reflector should be used for helping people instead of hurting them. Maria donned a crimson cape as well as the mantle of The Red Fury – a mysterious crime fighter, who can appear anywhere out of thin air…like a ghost.

THE FLEA

About

Doctor Albert Gæmelkrå was a brilliant and respected biochemist that worked for one of the biggest medical firms in the world, ExoFarm Industries. But Albert was also an introvert who lived in his mother’s basement. A portly man, whose right leg was so badly riddled with arthritis, that he had to wear a big, metallic leg brace in order to walk.

When his mother contracted a terminal illness, Albert turned to science to save her. He had a theory that an extremely rare species of ant, only found in a remote part of China, had a unique antibody capable of curing life-threatening diseases. So he started to develop a serum called Hercules, which could transfer those same healing properties from the insect to his mother.

When the president of ExoFarm found out, that Albert was carrying out secret human experiments on his own mother, he immediately terminated the project. The doctor begged and pleaded the C.E.O. to let him continue the treatments, but to no avail.

Albert was unable to save his mother. The grief-stricken doctor turned his back on the world and became a recluse…until the day a chubby 12-year-old boy stumbled into his backyard and got bitten by an ant from his laboratory. An ant injected with the Hercules-serum.

When Albert discovered, that Hercules had transformed the boy into a superhero, he used the serum to take the powers from a different insect…the flea. The serum removed the arthritis from his legs and made them a hundred times stronger, making him able to jump incredible high. But Albert also inherited the flea’s insatiable thirst for blood, the only thing that could sustain his powers.

Albert, who dubbed himself The Flea, tried to use his newfound powers to exact revenge on ExoFarm and everyone else who’d ever wronged him, but was thwarted by Antboy and sent off to Dragensborg Asylum.

The Flea is obsessed with destroying Antboy and uses his genius-level intelligence to concoct new elaborate schemes to entrap him.

SIDEKICK

About

To the outside world Wilhelm was that weird comic book geek who always talked in overbearing, sarcastic quips and didn’t seem to care much for other people. But in truth Wilhelm’s arrogant manner was only masking his own insecurities. His poor social skills alienated him from other people, and although he would never admit it, even to himself, he was very lonely.

Over time he had convinced himself, that friendship was an overrated concept, and that his enormous comic collection was the only company he would ever need. Then one day the bespectacled nerd suddenly started to suspect, that another boy at school, a chubby and goofy looking kid named Pelle, actually might have real superpowers!

Wilhelm put his disdain for socializing aside, explained his theory to Pelle and tried to persuade him, that he needed help from an expert. After a lot of convincing Pelle finally agreed to become a superhero (mostly because he thought, it would make him more popular). Wilhelm immediately started to test his abilities, equip him with hi-tec gadgetry and, most importantly, create a costume fit for a real life crime fighter – as well as Pelle’s egg-shaped physic.

Antboy was born. Pelle provided the brawn, while Wilhelm was the brains of the operation - at least if you asked him. While Antboy would patrol the streets of Middletown and fight bad guys, Wilhelm would mend and improve the battle damaged suits, build new gadgets, help solve crimes, coordinate, organize and provide intel via the AntCom. But Wilhelm dreamed of being more than Antboy’s sidekick. He wanted to be a superhero.

BEETLE BOYS

About

Mark and Allan were the two most feared bullies in Middletown. So much so, that the two teenage boys had been nicknamed The Terror Twins by the kids they tormented on a regular basis. Kids like Pelle and Wilhelm. But when Antboy put a stop to the despicable duo and humiliated them in front of the entire school, the two simpletons swore revenge on the chubby superhero and allegiance to his arch nemesis; The Flea.

Mark and Allan were bitten by a giant stag beetle, which was infected with the Hercules-toxin, and mutated into muscle-bound, armor plated humanoid insects with incredible strength…if not the I.Q. to match.

The Flea uses the beetle boys as his henchmen, but more often than not the dimwitted hooligans are too stupid to carry out his evil plans. And although The Trouble Twins are a force to be reckoned with, when it comes to brute strength and power, Antboy can usually outsmart them rather than fight them head on.

Anders Ølholm

Head Writer

National Film School of Denmark
Freelancer
olholmfilm@gmail.com
"Antboy, the first Danish superhero film, marked his feature film debut"

Anders Ølholm (b. 1983) graduated from the screenwriting program at The National Film School of Denmark in 2009.

Antboy, the first Danish superhero film, marked his feature film debut. The film premiered at the Toronto Film Festival and the Buster International Film Festival. In 2014 he penned the critically acclaimed hip hop drama Ækte Vare (Flow) as well as Antboy: Revenge of the Red Fury, which was selected for the Berlin Film Festival in the Generation Kplus category, and received the Danish Academy Award for best childrens film.

Anders is currently finishing the script for Antboy III, which will be in cinemas in February 2016. He has several other projects in development, such as an adaption of a novel by Jonas Bengtsson.

Flemming Schmidt

Flemming Schmidt (b. 1984) graduated from The Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts in 2011, majoring in Design for film and tv. Specializing in illustration and animation design he has since worked in multiple media, including children's books, computer games, poster art and more.

His debut in animation came with the 3d-animated short Piggy that aired on tv Christmas Eve in Denmark and Norway, spawning an illustrated children's book in 2012.

Since then he has illustrated more than 10 titles and is currently writing a series of his own, scheduled for 2015.

Other publications include cover art for World War Z (Danish book version) and for the acclaimed transmedia game Cloud Chamber. In 2014 his illustrations made it to the big screen with Antboy: Revenge of the Red Fury and the documentary Bad Boy Breathing.

Bo Ehrhardt graduated as producer from The National Film School of Denmark in 1993 and co-founded Nimbus Film with Birgitte Hald the same year. Nimbus Film has always been synonymous with developing talent and setting trends in the Danish film industry, playing a leading role in the Dogme-movement in the late 1990s, with award winning films such as The Celebration (Thomas Vinterberg) and Mifune (Søren Kragh-Jacobsen). Bo Ehrhardt is executive producer on all Nimbus Film productions, of which the latest are The Hour of the Lynx (Søren Kragh-Jacobsen), starring Sofie Gråbøl in the lead role, as well as Denmark’s first superhero Antboy, which premiered in the Danish cinemas in the autumn of 2013.

Bo Ehrhardt is also producing the TV-series The Bridge, of which season two was broadcast simultaneously in all five Nordic countries in the autumn of 2013. Season three is currently in post production. Nimbus Film has produced more than 50 feature films, including King’s Game (Nikolaj Arcel), Flame & Citron (Ole Chr. Petter Lindblad (born 1975) graduated from the Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm in 2000, where he received a Master in Media Production. Since then he has worked in different medias - digital TV, web, film and games - until going to the MEDIA-supported Media Business School in Spain in 2005, where he received a Master in European Audiovisual Management. During that program he ended up at Zentropa in Denmark, where he worked as assistant producer on the CGI-heavy adventure feature film Island of Lost Souls.

In January 2007 he started working at Copenhagen Bombay with CEO/founders Sarita Christensen and Anders Morgenthaler as a producer, mainly on animation projects, among others as producer for the feature animations The Apple and The Worm and The Great Bear, the short features Carsten and Gitte’s Movie Madness and The Secret of The Ice Flower, as well as the game project Beyond Beyond, and the Game and the TV-series The Pandas.

From June 2011 to March 2014 he ran the Swedish branch - CB Sverige AB - in Stockholm, that he built for the mother company Copenhagen Bombay and its ambitions for the Swedish market. He has recruited the teams and started projects for the company. There he produced Beyond Beyond, Sweden's first stereoscopic (3D) feature film ever, in the studio. And acted as co-producer on the 2D-animated film The Golden Horse.

Since April 2014 he runs his own production company, Snowcloud Films AB, with the ambition to create exciting, interesting and entertaining stories for childen and youths, with a special focus on animation. He recently finished a 30 minute animation film for SVTB titled Lea and the Forest Pirates by director Maria AVramova, and producing the live action/CGI feature film Siv Sleeps Astray with director Catti Edfeldt and Lena Hanno Clyne. He was selected by The Swedish Film Institute to be Sweden's Producer on the Move in Cannes 2014.
